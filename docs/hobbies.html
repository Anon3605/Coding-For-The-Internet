My Hobbies & Interests

Academics are just one part of who I am. Outside the classroom I'm drawn to things that make me think differently — whether that's dissecting military history, chasing down a new vulnerability write-up, or simply getting lost in my own thoughts on a quiet walk. These are the things that actually make up my days.

War Strategies & Military History

There's something endlessly fascinating about how battles are won and lost — not just through firepower, but through deception, timing, and information control. I read extensively about historical campaigns: Sun Tzu's principles, the blitzkrieg doctrine, Cold War proxy conflicts, and modern asymmetric warfare. I'm particularly drawn to the overlap between military strategy and cyber operations — both are ultimately about controlling the battlefield before the first shot is fired. Games like Hearts of Iron and Total War scratch the same itch interactively.

Cybersecurity & Zero-Day Research

Cybersecurity isn't just my field of study — it's genuinely what I think about in my spare time. I follow vulnerability disclosure feeds, read CVE write-ups, and dig into how zero-day exploits are discovered and weaponised. The cat-and-mouse dynamic between attackers and defenders is one of the most intellectually intense games being played right now. I spend time on platforms like Exploit-DB and follow researchers who publish responsible disclosure reports. My long-term goal is to contribute to that community — finding and reporting vulnerabilities before the wrong people do.

Music & Mood

I don't have a fixed genre — I have moods. Late at night debugging something, it's dark ambient or lo-fi hip-hop. On a productive afternoon, it's something with a driving beat. When I need to think slowly and clearly, it's jazz or classical. Music for me is less about fandom and more about atmosphere — the right track can completely change the texture of whatever I'm doing. I keep a rotating set of playlists for every mental state and rarely listen to the same thing two days in a row.

Movies + Junk Food (Twice a Month)

About twice a month I set aside a proper movie night — junk food mandatory, phone face-down, full commitment. There's something satisfying about treating it as a ritual rather than something you half-watch. I tend toward thrillers, war films, and anything with a clever plot structure. The junk food lineup usually involves chips, something fizzy, and whatever looks bad-for-me at the shop. No regrets.

Walking Alone & Getting Lost in Thought

Some of my best thinking happens not in front of a screen but on a slow, aimless walk through a park with no destination. No podcast, no music — just the ambient noise of the world and wherever my mind decides to go. It's the closest thing I have to meditation. I process problems differently when I'm moving; ideas that felt tangled at a desk suddenly untangle themselves mid-stride. I think everyone should try doing absolutely nothing productive for thirty minutes a day. Most people are surprised how much actually gets done.
